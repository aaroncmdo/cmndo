## Redundancy clusters — overlapping table families
**Summary:** I re-ran every row count and column-shape query against PROD and re-read all cited file:lines. The two flagship findings hold up strongly: the 3 trivial reminder "sent-log" tables are genuinely structurally identical (verified column-for-column), and the call-table family confirms `calls` as the unambiguous SSoT with two orphaned provider landing zones. However, three findings need correction. The `parteien` "read the wrong table, fix by repointing to claim_parties" recommendation is **refuted** at the data level — `claim_parties` holds only 72 `geschaedigter` rows and zero `gegner`/`kanzlei` rows, exactly the roles those readers query, so a naive repoint would still render empty; this is CMM-49 §A orphan-column territory, not a simple repoint. The billing position-table "same row, different parent FK, safely mergeable" claim is **overstated**: only 2 of the 4 position tables share `position_nr`, and `embed_abrechnung_positionen` has a completely different shape (anfrage/termin keys, `einzelpreis_eur`, no fall/claim FK). The notification, reminder-count, and `termine`/`zahlungseingaenge` findings are confirmed with minor numeric adjustments. Net: the finder's redundancy map is directionally correct, but two of its concrete "merge these" recommendations would lose data or break role semantics if executed as written.
